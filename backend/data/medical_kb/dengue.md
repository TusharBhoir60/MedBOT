# Dengue Fever

## Symptoms
- High fever (40°C / 104°F)
- Severe headache, particularly behind the eyes (retro-orbital pain)
- Muscle and joint pain (breakbone fever)
- Nausea and vomiting
- Skin rash appearing 2–5 days after fever onset
- Mild bleeding (nose bleed, gum bleed, easy bruising)
- Fatigue and malaise

## Risk Factors
- Living in or travelling to tropical/subtropical regions
- Prior dengue infection (increases risk of severe dengue)
- Age under 12 or over 65
- Pregnancy
- Immunocompromised status
- Monsoon season exposure (standing water breeding sites)

## Red Flags
- Severe abdominal pain or persistent vomiting
- Rapid breathing or difficulty breathing
- Bleeding gums, blood in vomit or stool
- Restlessness or lethargy
- Liver enlargement (>2 cm)
- Rapid decrease in platelet count
- Rising haematocrit with rapid decrease in platelet count
- Fluid accumulation (pleural effusion, ascites)

## Differential Diagnosis
- Chikungunya (similar joint pain but more prolonged)
- Zika virus (milder fever, conjunctivitis)
- Malaria (periodic fever, different pattern)
- Typhoid fever (sustained fever, abdominal symptoms)
- Leptospirosis (occupational exposure, jaundice)
- COVID-19 (respiratory symptoms predominate)

## Urgency
- **Routine**: Classic dengue fever without warning signs — supportive care, hydration, paracetamol.
- **Urgent**: Dengue with warning signs (abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, fluid accumulation) — requires close monitoring and possible hospitalisation.
- **Emergency**: Severe dengue (dengue hemorrhagic fever / dengue shock syndrome) — immediate ICU admission, IV fluid resuscitation, platelet monitoring.

## Source
WHO Dengue Guidelines for Diagnosis, Treatment, Prevention and Control (2009, updated 2012).
